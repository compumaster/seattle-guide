Link Light Rail

$3.00 one way from airport ~40 minutes from SEA

Sound Transit's 1 Line runs from SEA to downtown. Exit at Westlake Station — 3 blocks from convention center. From baggage claim, proceed to 4th floor parking garage, cross Skybridge 4. Free 'Train-to-Plane' shuttle runs along the 0.4-mile walkway. Trains every 8–15 min. Youth 18 and under ride free. Seniors/disabled $1.00 with RRFP. Pay via ORCA card, Transit GO Ticket app, or station machines.

🚌

King County Metro Bus

$2.75 per ride

Extensive bus network throughout Seattle and surrounding areas. Many routes stop near the convention center.

🚝

Seattle Monorail

$4.00 one way

Connects Westlake Center (near convention center) to Seattle Center (Space Needle area) in 2 minutes. Trains every 10 minutes. Note: As of Jan 2026, no ORCA E-purse transfers — separate fare required. Youth 6–18 and seniors $2.00, under 5 free. Hours: 7:30 AM–9 PM Mon–Thu, extended to 11 PM Fri–Sat.

🚇

Link 2 Line (Crosslake Connection) NEW!

~$3.00 one way 26 min to Bellevue Downtown

Opened March 28, 2026. World's first light rail across a floating bridge! Connects Seattle's International District to Mercer Island, Bellevue, and Redmond. Bellevue Downtown to Westlake Station in 26 minutes. Trains every 8 min during peak hours. Combined with the 1 Line, downtown tunnel trains arrive every 4 minutes.

📜 From Sawdust to Software

Seattle's growth has been defined by massive boom-and-bust cycles, reshaping the landscape — and even the physical elevation — of the city

The First Inhabitants

Long before European settlement, the Duwamish and Suquamish tribes inhabited the area. The city is named after Chief Seattle (Sealth), a prominent leader of both tribes.

The Great Seattle Fire (1889)

A glue pot boiled over in a carpentry shop, starting a fire that destroyed the central business district. Residents rebuilt using brick and stone, raising the street level — creating the famous 'Seattle Underground' that tourists can still visit today.

Klondike Gold Rush (1897)

When gold was discovered in the Yukon, Seattle marketed itself as the 'Gateway to Alaska' and became the primary outfitting point for miners. The massive influx of wealth transformed Seattle from a struggling pioneer town into a thriving metropolis overnight.

The World's Fair (1962)

Officially called the Century 21 Exposition, this event left the city with its most iconic landmarks: the Space Needle, the Monorail, and the Seattle Center.

The Tech Boom (1980s–Present)

The rise of Microsoft in the 80s and 90s, followed by the massive expansion of Amazon in the 2010s, created a huge influx of wealth and development, completely transforming neighborhoods like South Lake Union.

🌋 Fire and Ice: Seattle's Geography

Nestled between mountain ranges and deep waterways, sitting on the Pacific Ring of Fire

Mount Rainier

Volcano / Mountain

Towering at 14,411 feet, Mount Rainier is an active stratovolcano that dominates the Seattle skyline on clear days. The most heavily glaciated peak in the contiguous US and a crown jewel for hikers and climbers. About 60 miles southeast of the city.

Mount St. Helens

Volcano

On May 18, 1980, Mount St. Helens experienced a catastrophic eruption — the most destructive volcanic event in US history, reducing its height by 1,300 feet. Today, visitors can drive to the Johnston Ridge Observatory to look directly into the crater. About 175 miles south of Seattle.

The Cascade Range

Mountain Range

A major mountain range stretching from British Columbia to Northern California. Acts as a massive wall east of Seattle, trapping moisture from the Pacific Ocean and contributing to the region's famously green, rainy climate.

Puget Sound

Waterway

A complex system of deep, glacier-carved saltwater channels connected to the Pacific Ocean. The reason Seattle became a major maritime hub, and home to rich marine life including the region's famous pods of Orca whales.

💡 Local Insights

🧬 Life Sciences

Greater Seattle supports 1,180+ life science companies generating $13.7 billion GDP. The South Lake Union neighborhood houses Fred Hutch Cancer Center, Allen Institute, and UW Medicine alongside Amazon HQ.

☕ Coffee Capital

Seattle is the birthplace of Starbucks (1971) and the epicenter of America's specialty coffee culture. Don't miss the original Starbucks at Pike Place and the 15,000 sq ft Reserve Roastery.

🎸 Grunge Heritage

Seattle gave the world grunge music — Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains. Visit MoPOP's music exhibits and the venue scene in Capitol Hill.

💻 Tech Hub

Home to Amazon, Microsoft (Redmond), Boeing, and thousands of startups. The new 2 Line light rail connects Seattle to the Eastside tech corridor.

🐟 Pike Place Market

Pike Place Market (1907) implemented a limited-access vehicle pilot for 2026 — pedestrian-only on Pike Place between 10 AM and 4 PM. Even more walkable than before!

🛡️ Safety

Downtown and tourist areas are generally safe during daylight. Standard urban awareness advised, especially around Pioneer Square at night.
